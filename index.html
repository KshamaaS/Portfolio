About Me

Hi! I'm Kshamaa—a Data Science Master's student at Columbia who believes the best AI systems are built by people who understand both algorithms and the human experience.

I spent two years at Ecolab building production GenAI systems (think: Claude API on Databricks processing millions of records), and now I'm diving deeper into computational biology, LLMs, and agentic AI. My work spans neural networks, transformers, pipelines, and large-scale data engineering—but what excites me most is using these tools to solve problems that actually matter 🧠.

Here's the plot twist: I'm also a published author ✍️. My poetry and short story collections - A Memo of Memories and World With Words are on Amazon, because apparently, I can't decide if I want to train neural networks or write sonnets. Why not both?

When I'm not coding or writing, I'm probably reading, painting, or going on adventures with my dog 🐕— my most reliable debugging companion and the one who never judges my variable names.
